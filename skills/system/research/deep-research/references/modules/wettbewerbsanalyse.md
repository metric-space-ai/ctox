# Module: Wettbewerbsanalyse / Competitive Analysis

Use this module for competitor mapping, product comparison, pricing/packaging analysis, positioning, moat assessment, and strategic whitespace analysis.

## Decision Frame

State the decision as: "How is the competitive landscape structured, where are the defensible gaps, and what positioning or product strategy follows from the evidence?"

Extract:

- product/category boundary
- target market/geography
- competitor types: direct, adjacent, substitute, incumbent/manual workaround
- buyer/user
- evaluation dimensions
- strategic question: build, buy, partner, enter, reposition, or differentiate

## Evidence Matrix

Build a matrix with these columns:

- competitor
- category / archetype
- target customer
- positioning
- core features
- pricing / packaging
- proof points
- distribution/channel
- strengths
- weaknesses
- moat / lock-in
- evidence confidence

## Search Coverage

Cover:

- official product pages, docs, demos, pricing, changelogs
- customer reviews and forums where relevant
- case studies and references
- job posts and partner pages for strategic signals
- patents, standards, integrations, and certifications
- filings/investor materials for public companies
- traffic/download/review proxies only with caveats

## Default Report Structure

1. Executive Summary
2. Category Definition and Scope
3. Methodology and Source Quality
4. Competitor Landscape
5. Feature and Capability Matrix
6. Pricing and Packaging
7. Positioning and Messaging
8. Moats, Channels, and Partnerships
9. Strategic Gaps and Threats
10. Recommendation
11. References

## Completion Criteria

- Separate direct competitors from substitutes.
- Include evidence-backed feature/pricing comparisons.
- Identify defensible differentiation, not just missing features.
- Flag stale, inferred, or unverifiable competitor data.
