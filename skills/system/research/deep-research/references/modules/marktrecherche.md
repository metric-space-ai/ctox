# Module: Marktrecherche / Market Research

Use this module for market sizing, customer discovery, buying behavior, pricing, channel analysis, regulatory/commercial context, and go-to-market research.

## Decision Frame

State the decision as: "Is this market attractive enough to enter or prioritize, who buys, why now, and what evidence supports the opportunity size and route to market?"

Extract:

- target geography
- buyer/user/persona
- product/service category
- time horizon
- revenue model
- market maturity
- required output: TAM/SAM/SOM, segment map, GTM recommendation, or investment memo

## Evidence Matrix

Build a matrix with these columns:

- segment
- buyer and user
- job-to-be-done / pain
- budget owner
- purchase trigger
- market size evidence
- growth drivers
- adoption barriers
- regulation/procurement constraints
- pricing/packaging signals
- channels
- source confidence

## Search Coverage

Cover:

- industry reports and public extracts
- regulator/statistical sources
- company filings and investor presentations
- procurement portals and tenders
- pricing pages and marketplaces
- customer forums/reviews where relevant
- analyst commentary only as secondary evidence
- adjacent-market proxies when direct data is missing

## Default Report Structure

1. Executive Summary
2. Market Definition and Scope
3. Methodology and Source Quality
4. Market Size and Growth
5. Customer Segments and Buyer Behavior
6. Demand Drivers and Barriers
7. Pricing and Business Models
8. Channels and Go-to-Market Options
9. Regulatory / Procurement Context
10. Opportunity Assessment and Recommendation
11. References

## Completion Criteria

- Distinguish measured data from inferred estimates.
- Give ranges for uncertain market sizing.
- Explain proxy assumptions.
- Identify practical entry points and disqualifying barriers.
